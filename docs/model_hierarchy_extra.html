Instead of focusing on individual partnerships we can use a hierarhical model to explore other effects.

We will look at:

  1. Bank Type

    Conclusions:
    There was a fixed effect of 0.25 percentage point increase in Credit Card Plans-30-89 DAYS P/D % if a bank was a Large Credit Card Bank. The std.err at 0.10 suggests that we still can improve the model as there is some uncertainty about the true value of the coefficient.

  2. Numeric proxy for Bank Type - measure Credit Card Loans, % Gross LN&LS (UBPRE425). Large Credit Card Banks will have higher proportions in credit card loans compared with other loan types e.g., personal, car and mortgage loans.

    Conclusions:
    Our term is UBPRE425.log.diff. The log difference is a good approximation of percentage change when changes are small. Assuming this, we can say a 1% increase in Credit Card Loans, % Gross LN&LS results is a fixed effect of 0.65 percentage point increase in Credit Card Plans-30-89 DAYS P/D %. The std.err term is 0.13 indicating this estimate is quite precise. The random effect of UBPRE425.log.diff was not distinguishable from zero i.e. the contribution to the variability of our target measure could be by chance alone.

  3. NAICS type. If we group partnerships by their NAICS level 2 will we see an effect?

    Conclusions:
    Compared with having no partner, we see a fixed effect of 0.08 percent point increase (with std.err 0.02) in Credit Card Plans-30-89 DAYS P/D % for Manufacturng companies (NAICS2 = 33). There was no effect for retail companies. Because we focused on partnerships that had partner events our partner list is limited (see Table 12.5). For Manufacturing companies we have a sample size of 2 - Apple and GM. This could be a future area of study where we look at all partnership cards a bank issues which can be found in the Terms of Credit Card Plans Survey (2022) and research when these partnerships were established.
